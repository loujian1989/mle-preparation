### Netflix

#### TC
| Level | Base | Stock Options (annualized value) | Total TC |
|---|---|---|---|
| E5 (Senior) | $212K–$245K | ~$278K (option-based) | **$523K** (median) |
| E6 (Staff) | $260K–$280K | ~$420K+ | **$705K** (median) |

*levels.fyi, March 2026; Glassdoor Senior MLE median ~$245K base*

#### Comp Component Breakdown
- Compensation model is **fundamentally different** from peers:
  - **Everything is paid as cash salary** — this is the default and the entire offer
  - No bonus, no automatic RSU grant
  - Each year you may voluntarily elect to receive a portion of your salary as stock options instead of cash (e.g., swap $100K of salary for $100K worth of options)
  - Most employees hold some mix; the "TC" figures on levels.fyi reflect total cash + option value for those who elect stock
  - Options chosen this way are immediately vested; 10-year exercise window

#### RSU Vesting Schedule
- **Schedule type: N/A — Netflix is effectively an all-cash offer by default**
- Your entire comp is paid as salary (cash). There is no automatic equity grant.
- Each year you may *elect* to convert a portion of your cash salary into stock options — it is voluntary, not the default
- If you elect options: those options are **immediately vested at grant** (no cliff, no schedule, no wait)
- 10-year exercise window on any options you hold (vs. standard 90-day post-termination window elsewhere — significant portability advantage if you leave)
- Executives (2024+): separate mandatory time-based RSUs and performance RSUs exist, but do not apply to IC roles

#### RSU Refresh Policy — N/A (Option model)
- Employees reallocate their annual stock option percentage each year
- No "refresh grants" in traditional sense; compensation is recalibrated annually
- Annual market-rate review keeps base competitive

#### Signing Bonus
- Not standard; Netflix philosophy is to incorporate all value into the recurring high base
- Some negotiated cases may include relocation/transition support

#### Key Benefits
| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| 401(k) | Up to 4% match (some conflicting reports; verify in offer) |
| Health | Comprehensive medical, dental, vision |
| Parental Leave | 4–8 months paid (birth or adoption); pulled back from original unlimited first-year policy |
| PTO | Unlimited |
| Home Office | Stipends available; hybrid/remote supported by role |
| Other | Hack-days; strong L&D culture |

#### WLB
- **Glassdoor WLB:** 3.7/5.0 (best of the 9)
- Results-oriented; no time tracking; flexible hours
- Performance bar is extremely high — "Keeper Test" culture (managers ask "would I fight to keep this person?")
- No formal on-call policy disclosed; team-dependent

#### Job Security 🟡
- Feb 2026: Small product division layoffs (<1% of ~6K division)
- 2026: Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition in progress ($83B deal); $2–3B cost savings targeted by Year 3
- Acquisition-driven uncertainty: role scope, reporting lines, and culture may shift post-merger
- Prior to 2026: relatively stable headcount vs. FAANG peers

#### Background ROI ★★★★☆ 4/5
- **Netflix is actively building its own in-house ad server (launched Canada 2024, US rollout 2025)** — this is a greenfield ads ML opportunity, not a mature system
- MLE L5 Ads job postings explicitly ask for "optimization methods, ad server algorithms and bidder systems" — direct match to DoorDash/Amazon background
- Ad tier at 40M+ users; ad revenue targeting $1.5B+ in 2025 and doubling again — ads ML is strategically critical, not a side project
- Prior to 2024: Netflix relied on Microsoft as exclusive ad server; now building in-house from scratch — high ownership, early mover advantage for ML engineers joining now
- World-class ML infrastructure (Metaflow, Keystone/Kafka/Flink — 500B+ events/day) applies to ads pipelines
- WBD acquisition creates near-term uncertainty on role scope and reporting structure
- PhD mechanism design + auction/bidding background is a direct match for the bidder systems they are building

#### RSU Refresh Policy ★★☆☆☆
- No traditional refresh grants
- Instead, employees reallocate their desired stock % each quarter
- Total compensation stays flat until promotion or renegotiation
- No performance multiplier mechanism

#### Signing Bonus

- Monthly drumbeat creates morale and uncertainty concerns
- CEO signaling AI-first expectations creates implicit headcount pressure

#### Background ROI ★★★☆☆ 3/5
- Strong culture for clean architecture and greenfield engineering (SOLID principles, DDD)
- Merchant ML focus: fraud, demand forecasting, capital products — adjacent to but not core to ads/auction work
- PhD mechanism design not a primary signal for Shopify's product roadmap

### 6b. Startup Equity Value Model

Understanding startup option value requires modeling the full exit scenario — not just the grant size.

**Key concepts:**

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **409A valuation** | IRS-approved FMV of common stock; sets your strike (exercise) price |
| **Preferred price** | Price VCs paid; typically 2–3× the 409A |
| **Liquidation preference** | VCs get paid first; standard 1× non-participating preferred (investors get 1× money back, then convert to common) |
| **Strike price** | What you pay to exercise options; you profit on the spread above this |
| **AMT exposure** | Exercising ISOs triggers Alternative Minimum Tax on the spread (FMV − strike) even if you can't sell |
| **83(b) election** | File within 30 days of grant to start capital gains clock early; critical for large grants |
| **QSBS** | Qualified Small Business Stock: up to $10M (or 10× basis) in capital gains excluded from federal tax if held 5+ years |
| **90-day window** | Standard post-termination option exercise window; negotiate extended window (2–10 years) upfront |

**Exit scenario analysis (illustrative — using Rokt as example):**

| Exit Scenario | Company Valuation | Common per Share | Strike ($35) | Profit per 1,000 options |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acqui-hire | $300M | ~$5 | $35 | ❌ Underwater |
| Down-round IPO | $2B | ~$25 | $35 | ❌ Underwater |
| Current valuation IPO | $7.9B | ~$70 | $35 | $35K profit |
| Bull case IPO | $12.1B | ~$100 | $35 | $65K profit |
| Moonshot | $20B | ~$165 | $35 | $130K profit |

> Note: Assumes 1× non-participating preferred, fully diluted share count ~120M (illustrative). Actual share counts require cap table access.

**Exercise strategy:**
- **Early exercise + 83(b):** If 409A is low and you believe in the company, exercise immediately after grant, file 83(b) within 30 days → starts long-term capital gains clock (15–20% rate vs. 37% ordinary income)
- **Wait for liquidity:** If you can't afford AMT exposure, wait for IPO/tender → pay ordinary income tax on spread but avoid capital outlay
- **QSBS window:** Hold for 5 years post-exercise; first $10M gain is federal tax-free (Section 1202); applies to companies with <$50M assets at time of grant
